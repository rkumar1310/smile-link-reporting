# Section 2: Personal Summary

You're dealing with both missing teeth and some cosmetic concerns about your smile. This is a very common combination, and there are well-established treatments that address both aspects together. The goal is a result that's comfortable, natural-looking, and reliable.

# Section 3: Your Situation

When you're facing both functional gaps and aesthetic issues, it's natural to feel uncertain about how to proceed. The good news is that this type of situation is very familiar to dental professionals—it's one of the most common combinations they treat.

Your mouth is capable of supporting a comprehensive restoration. Nothing about your situation is unusual or cause for concern. The treatment approach for combined functional and aesthetic work is well-documented and highly predictable.

Many people in similar situations have achieved excellent results. The process involves multiple steps, but each step is straightforward and manageable.

# Section 4: Possible Treatment Direction

For combined concerns, a coordinated approach is typically recommended. This means addressing the missing teeth and the aesthetic issues in one planned treatment, so everything works together in the end.

# Section 5: Treatment Option — Implants Combined with Veneers or Crowns

**What this involves**
Missing teeth are replaced with implants and crowns. Visible teeth that need improvement are treated with veneers or crowns. Everything is planned together for a unified result.

**What you can expect**
A comprehensive restoration that addresses both function and appearance.

**Why this is considered reliable**
- Both implants and veneers/crowns are proven treatments
- The combined approach has a strong track record
- Results are predictable when properly planned
- The outcome is designed to last

**What to know about the process**
- Treatment proceeds in organized phases
- Each phase is manageable
- Recovery between phases is typically smooth
- The team coordinates everything to align properly

**This is often chosen by people who...**
Want a comprehensive solution that they can count on.

# Section 6: Expected Results & Comfort

The goal is a smile that works well and looks natural. You'll be able to eat comfortably and smile confidently. The treatment is designed to feel stable and reliable—something you don't have to worry about after it's complete.

# Section 7: Duration of the Process

- Combined treatment: typically 4–7 months

The timeline is spread out to allow for proper healing and coordination.

# Section 8: Recovery Time & Impact on Daily Life

**Implants**
- Recovery: 2–5 days of mild discomfort
- Daily life: Most people resume activities quickly

**Veneers or crowns**
- Recovery: 1–2 days
- Daily life: Minimal disruption

# Section 9: Cost Indication (Guide Prices)

- Implant with crown: approximately €2,200 – €2,500 per tooth
- Veneers or crowns: approximately €900 – €1,300 per tooth

Your dental team can provide specific estimates for your case.

# Section 10: Considerations for Your Decision

- How important is addressing both function and appearance?
- Are you comfortable with a phased approach?
- What questions would help you feel more confident?

Writing down your thoughts can help prepare for your consultation.

There's no pressure to decide immediately. When you're ready:

Combined treatments like this are very successful. Whatever you decide, you'll be supported by experienced professionals.
