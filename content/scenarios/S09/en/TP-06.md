# Section 2: Personal Summary

You have no teeth in one jaw. Treatment options exist that can restore function and comfort. What you choose is entirely your decision.

# Section 3: Your Situation

Complete edentulousness in one jaw affects eating, speaking, and comfort. You may have adapted to this situation, or you may find it significantly limiting. How much this matters, and what to do about it, is personal.

A dental professional can explain options and outcomes. The decision of whether, when, and how to proceed remains yours.

# Section 4: Treatment Directions

Two approaches are commonly offered. The information below is meant to inform your thinking.

# Section 5: Option 1 — All-on-4/All-on-6 Fixed Bridge

**What this offers you**
- A completely fixed solution
- Maximum stability
- No removal required

**What to consider**
- Higher cost
- Surgical procedure
- Multi-month process

# Section 6: Option 2 — Snap-on Denture on Implants

**What this offers you**
- Significantly improved stability
- More affordable option
- Removable for cleaning

**What to consider**
- Still requires daily removal
- Less fixed feeling
- May need adjustments over time

# Section 7-11: [Practical Information]

Duration: 4-9 months | Recovery: 5-10 days | Cost: €4,000-€20,000

**Your next steps are entirely up to you.** There is no external timeline or pressure. Take whatever time feels right to decide.
